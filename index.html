01  Concept

Encrypt your memory locally, park it somewhere durable

Encrypt the notes and chats you feed to AI on your own machine, turn them into a queryable second brain, and park the ciphertext permanently on Arweave — pay once, stored forever. Three layers: Cipher, Brain, Store.

Steps 1–3 run on your machine — only encrypted data ever reaches Arweave.
02  Problem

Your brain now lives on someone else’s server

The more you offload to AI, the more your second brain lives inside a platform — deletable, alterable, and readable by whoever runs it. Your most personal data is the one you own least.

Same second brain, two custody models — only the encrypted-on-Arweave one is truly yours.
03  Solution

Encrypt it, keep the key, and park it somewhere no one can take it down

Three layers in one tool: Cipher encrypts on-device, Brain makes your memory queryable, and Store parks the ciphertext on Arweave — a one-time payment for permanence, beyond any single party's reach.

Cipher + Brain run on your machine; Store parks the encrypted result on Arweave.
Cipher
Client-side encryption keyed to you — the network only ever holds ciphertext.
Brain
An AI second brain over your own corpus — memory you can ask, not just store.
Store
Arweave pay-once permanence — no server to babysit; a fresh machine restores with just the locator + your key.
04  Why Arweave

A permanence you pay for once, not uptime you have to maintain

A backup must outlive your attention. Arweave funds perpetual replication from a one-time upload fee — no contracts to renew, no server to keep alive — and getting the bytes back is a plain HTTP fetch from any gateway.

Pay-once permanence
One upload fee funds an endowment that pays for replication — the archive survives your neglect by design.
Recovery = plain HTTP
No wallet, no SDK — a fresh machine restores with just the tx id and your key; any public gateway serves the bytes.
Ciphertext only
The network is public and permanent, but it only ever holds bytes that your key alone can decrypt.
05  Status & Roadmap

The whole loop runs today — encrypt, park, restore

The Cipher layer round-trips against real second-brain data, the Brain layer synthesizes nightly, and real snapshots have been parked on Arweave and pulled back from a public gateway.

STEP 1Cipher layer — a key only you hold
cipher-brain encrypts each snapshot with age (X25519) to your public key; the private identity stays off the always-on machine. The round-trip has been validated end-to-end against real gbrain data, and a different identity cannot restore it.
# encrypt on your machine — storage never sees plaintext $ cipher-brain snapshot --pg "$PG" --dir ~/.gbrain --out brain.age
STEP 2Brain layer — a second brain we already run
We already run a personal second brain (GBrain) nightly on a local machine: it ingests our AI conversations across projects, embeds them, and synthesizes links, then serves hybrid search plus LLM reasoning over the whole corpus.
# the brain we run every night — locally $ gbrain think "what did we decide about X?"
STEP 3Store — park it permanently on Arweave
push uploads the ciphertext via a bundler (payable with ETH/USDC, under 100 KB free); pull is a plain gateway fetch that needs no wallet and no SDK. Proven against the real network: a snapshot slice was uploaded, retrieved from a public gateway, and decrypted.
# pay once — the network keeps it forever $ cipher-brain push --in brain.age --backend turbo
